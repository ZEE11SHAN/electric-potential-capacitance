Electric Potential

Electric potential is the amount of electric potential energy per unit charge at a point in a static electric field. It is usually measured in volts. The electric potential \(V\) at a point in space is given by:

\(V = \frac{U}{q}\)

where \(U\) is the electric potential energy and \(q\) is the charge.

Electric Potential Due to Point Charges

The electric potential \(V\) due to a point charge \(q\) at a distance \(r\) is given by:

\(V = k_e \frac{q}{r}\)

Electric Dipole Potential

The potential \(V\) due to an electric dipole at a point in space is given by:

\(V = \frac{1}{4\pi \epsilon_0} \frac{\vec{p} \cdot \hat{r}}{r^2}\)

Electric Potential Energy

Electric potential energy is the energy a charge has due to its position in an electric field. The potential energy \(U\) of a charge \(q\) at a point where the electric potential is \(V\) is given by:

\(U = qV\)

Potential Energy of a System of Charges

For two point charges \(q_1\) and \(q_2\) separated by a distance \(r\), the electric potential energy is given by:

\(U = k_e \frac{q_1 q_2}{r}\)

Equipotential Surfaces

Equipotential surfaces are surfaces where the electric potential is the same everywhere. Since the electric potential is constant on an equipotential surface, no work is required to move a charge along such a surface.

Equipotential Surfaces of Point Charges

For a point charge, equipotential surfaces are concentric spheres centered on the charge.

Capacitance

Capacitance is the ability of a system to store electric charge per unit voltage. The capacitance \(C\) is given by:

\(C = \frac{Q}{V}\)

Parallel Plate Capacitor

The capacitance of a parallel plate capacitor is given by:

\(C = \epsilon_0 \frac{A}{d}\)

where \(A\) is the area of the plates and \(d\) is the separation between them.

Types of Capacitors

There are various types of capacitors used in electronic circuits, each with different characteristics and applications.

Fixed Capacitors

  • Ceramic Capacitors: Used for high-frequency applications.
  • Electrolytic Capacitors: Used for large capacitance values.
  • Film Capacitors: Used for stable and precise capacitance values.

Variable Capacitors

Used in applications where the capacitance needs to be adjusted, such as in tuning circuits.

Capacitors in Circuits

Capacitors can be connected in series or parallel configurations to achieve desired capacitance values in circuits.

Capacitors in Series

The total capacitance \(C_{\text{total}}\) of capacitors in series is given by:

\(\frac{1}{C_{\text{total}}} = \frac{1}{C_1} + \frac{1}{C_2} + \ldots + \frac{1}{C_n}\)

Capacitors in Parallel

The total capacitance \(C_{\text{total}}\) of capacitors in parallel is given by:

\(C_{\text{total}} = C_1 + C_2 + \ldots + C_n\)

Energy Stored in Capacitors

The energy \(U\) stored in a capacitor is given by:

\(U = \frac{1}{2} CV^2\)

Energy Density

The energy density (energy per unit volume) in a capacitor is given by:

\(u = \frac{1}{2} \epsilon E^2\)

where \(\epsilon\) is the permittivity of the dielectric material and \(E\) is the electric field strength.

Applications of Capacitors

Capacitors have numerous applications in electronic circuits, including filtering, coupling, and energy storage.

Capacitors in Electronic Circuits

  • Filtering: Used to remove unwanted frequencies from signals.
  • Coupling and Decoupling: Used to connect different stages of a circuit while blocking DC components.

Advanced Applications

  • Capacitive Touchscreens: Used in modern touch-sensitive devices.
  • Medical Devices: Used in various diagnostic and therapeutic equipment.
